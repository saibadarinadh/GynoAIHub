Frequently Asked Questions

What is PCOS and what are its common symptoms?
PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is a hormonal disorder common among women of reproductive age. Common symptoms include:
  • Irregular periods
  • Excess androgen levels leading to acne and excess hair growth
  • Weight gain
  • Difficulty getting pregnant
  • Multiple small cysts on the ovaries
How often should I get a cervical cancer screening?
The general guidelines for cervical cancer screening are:
  • Ages 21-29: Pap test every 3 years
  • Ages 30-65: Pap test and HPV test every 5 years, or Pap test alone every 3 years
  • Over 65: May stop screening if previous tests were normal
However, your doctor might recommend a different schedule based on your personal health history.
What should I expect during my first gynecological visit?
Your first gynecological visit typically includes:
  • Discussion about your medical history and menstrual cycle
  • General health check including weight, blood pressure, etc.
  • Discussion about any concerns you may have
  • Information about preventive care
  • A physical exam may or may not be necessary depending on your age and circumstances
What are the early signs of pregnancy?
Common early signs of pregnancy include:
  • Missed period
  • Breast tenderness
  • Nausea/morning sickness
  • Fatigue
  • Frequent urination
  • Mood changes
If you experience these symptoms and suspect pregnancy, take a home pregnancy test or consult your healthcare provider.
How can I maintain good reproductive health?
To maintain good reproductive health:
  • Schedule regular check-ups with your gynecologist
  • Practice safe sex and use protection
  • Maintain a healthy diet and exercise routine
  • Stay up to date with vaccinations (including HPV)
  • Practice good hygiene
  • Track your menstrual cycle
  • Seek medical attention if you notice unusual symptoms
